About

I am a graduate of Southern Utah University (see link on left) with his degree in Computer Science. My career goals include specializing in mobile applications, Internet application, and authoring microcontroller programs.

I enjoy classic cars, for which I owns three. A 1954 Chevrolet Belair 4 door, 1954 Chevrolet 3100 Deluxe Cab pickup, and a 1983 Mercedes-Benz 380SL Roadster. I have discovered the fun of programming microcontrollers. Currently working with the Microchip controllers.

One of my hobbies is making stained glass windows. There is a certain satisfacting in deliberately breaking glass. The stained glass window on the left is a reproduction – copy – of a windows for the Boulder Colorado Freemasons from their original 1894 lodge building that was located Pearl Street, now part of the Pearl Street walking district. The building burned to the ground in 1945, with remnants of only 2 stained glass windows from that building still in their possession — though in very rough condition. This windows is currently on display at Boulder Lodge #14 Masonic Lodge building in Boulder, Colorado.

The window on the right was inspired by architect Frank Lloyd Wright's design style called "Prairie School". This particular window is styled after the windows that are in what is "The Ennis House"— one of his designs. The Ennis House has been featured in several movies, most notably the "13th Floor" and interior shots of the Meereen Palace in the "Game Of Thrones". .
