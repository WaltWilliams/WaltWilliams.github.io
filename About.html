About

Walt L. Williams is a Computer Science student in his final semester at Southern Utah University. It is his career goals to specialize in mobile applications, Internet application, and embedded systems.

Prior to entering College Walt had a career in Circuit Board Design, which he did for 12 years until these jobs were exported to China and India. In spite of really enjoying his work he was planning to start work on a Computer Science Bachelors degree anyway. Even though college is more difficult for an older person, Walt never lost sight of his goal, enduring many set-backs. Walt is looking forward to graduation and having a life again.

Walt enjoys classic cars, for which he owns three. A 1954 Chevrolet Belair 4 door, 1954 Chevrolet 3100 Deluxe Cab pickup, and a 1983 Mercedes-Benz 380SL Roadster.

Walt also enjoys the hobby of making stained glass windows. One of Walt's stained glass windows is a reproduction – copy – of a windows for the Boulder Freemasons from their original 1895 lodge building on Pearl Street, now part of the Pearl Street walking district. The building burned to the ground in 1945, with only remnants of 2 remaining stained glass windows from that building still in their possession. This windows is currently on display at Boulder Lodge #14 Masonic Lodge building in Boulder, Colorado.

The window on the right is inspired by Frank Lloyd Wright's design style called "Prairie School". This particular window is styled after the windows in what is called the Ennis House, which is featured in several movies, most notably the "13th Floor" and interior shots of the Meereen Palace in the "Game Of Thrones" .
